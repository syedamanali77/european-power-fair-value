The naive same-week-hour approach performed poorly with an MAE of around 35.4 MWh. In contrast, the hist gradient boosting model had a lower MAE of around 18.9 MWh.

A comparison of the week/month means to the prompt month or quarter forecast suggests that there is room for improvement in capturing seasonal patterns.

The QA summary shows that there are no duplicate timestamps and very little missing data (less than 0.01%). The IQR outlier fraction is relatively low, indicating that most values are within a reasonable range.

The forecasting delivery mean next week and month forecasts suggest that the model can capture some seasonal patterns, but may struggle with longer-term predictions.
